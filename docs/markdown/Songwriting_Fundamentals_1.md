# Songwriting Fundamentals

The complete guide to writing songs — from idea to finished composition.

---

## What Makes a Great Song

A great song has:
- **A strong hook** — Something memorable that sticks
- **Emotional connection** — Makes you feel something
- **Clear structure** — Takes you on a journey
- **Authenticity** — Feels real, not forced
- **Repeatability** — You want to hear it again

**The secret:** Great songs feel both familiar AND surprising.

---

## The Building Blocks

### The Core Elements

Every song needs:

| Element | What It Does | Priority |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Melody** | The tune you sing/hum | Essential |
| **Lyrics** | The words and meaning | Essential (for songs with vocals) |
| **Chords/Harmony** | The musical foundation | Essential |
| **Rhythm** | The groove and feel | Essential |
| **Structure** | How sections are organized | Essential |
| **Hook** | The memorable part | Critical for commercial songs |

### Where to Start

**There's no "right" way. Common approaches:**

| Starting Point | Best For | Process |
|----------------|----------|---------|
| Lyrics first | Personal/meaningful songs | Write words → find melody |
| Melody first | Catchy/melodic songs | Hum tune → add words |
| Chords first | Vibe/mood-driven songs | Play progression → melody over top |
| Beat/groove first | Rhythmic/dance songs | Build groove → melody/lyrics |
| Hook first | Commercial songs | Write hook → build around it |
| Title first | Concept songs | Start with phrase → develop |

**Try different approaches** — you'll find what works for you.

## The Songwriting Process

### Stage 1: Capture Ideas

**Always be collecting:**
- Voice memos (hum melodies, sing ideas)
- Notes app (lyrics, phrases, titles)
- Record jamming sessions
- Write down random thoughts

**Don't judge, just capture.** Edit later.

### Stage 2: Develop the Seed

**Take one idea and expand:**
- If you have a melody → what words fit?
- If you have lyrics → what melody serves them?
- If you have chords → what melody lives there?
- If you have a title → what's the story?

### Stage 3: Find the Hook

**Ask yourself:**
- What's the most memorable part?
- What would someone hum after hearing this?
- What's the one line/melody that sums it up?

**Build the song around your hook.**

### Stage 4: Build Structure

**Arrange your sections:**
- Where does it start?
- How does it build?
- Where's the peak?
- How does it end?

### Stage 5: Refine and Edit

**Kill your darlings:**
- Cut what doesn't serve the song
- Simplify where possible
- Make every word/note count
- Get outside feedback

## Finding Inspiration

### When You're Stuck

**Constraints spark creativity:**
- Write in a key you never use
- Write with only 3 chords
- Write in a genre you don't know
- Set a 20-minute timer
- Write from someone else's perspective

### Inspiration Sources

| Source | How to Use It |
|--------|---------------|
| Personal experience | Write what you know/feel |
| Other people's stories | Observe, empathize, imagine |
| Books/movies/art | Respond to other creative work |
| Titles and phrases | Start with an interesting phrase |
| Emotion | Start with how you want to feel |
| Musical ideas | Let the music guide the words |

### The Idea Bank

**Keep a running list of:**
- Interesting titles
- Phrases you hear
- Emotions you want to explore
- Stories worth telling
- Musical ideas (voice memos)

**When you sit down to write, you have options.**

## Co-Writing

### Why Co-Write

- Different perspectives
- Someone to bounce ideas off
- Accountability (scheduled sessions)
- Learn new techniques
- Often faster than solo writing

### Co-Writing Tips

**Before the session:**
- Discuss what you want to write
- Share examples/references
- Come with some ideas (but hold loosely)

**During the session:**
- Say "yes, and..." more than "no"
- Build on each other's ideas
- Take breaks when stuck
- Record everything

**After the session:**
- Decide on splits (usually equal)
- Determine who does what next
- Set deadline for follow-up

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Over-thinking | Never finishing | Set time limits, accept imperfection |
| Under-developing | Ideas stay shallow | Push past first idea to find better |
| No hook | Nothing memorable | Find the thing people will remember |
| Too complex | Hard to follow | Simplify — less is more |
| Too derivative | Sounds like copy | Add your unique perspective |
| Ignoring structure | Doesn't flow | Use proven structures as foundation |
| Not finishing | Pile of unfinished songs | Commit to finishing, even if imperfect |

## The Finish Line

### What "Done" Looks Like

**A finished song has:**
- Complete lyrics (all sections)
- Clear melody (singable, memorable)
- Defined structure (verse, chorus, etc.)
- Harmonic foundation (chords decided)
- A working title

**It doesn't need:**
- Perfect production
- Final arrangement
- Professional recording

**Demo quality is fine. Finish the song first.**

### Version Control

**Save your progress:**
- Date your files (Song_Title_2024-01-15.wav)
- Keep old versions
- Note what changed between versions
- Know when to stop editing

## Practice and Growth

### Daily Habits

**Write regularly:**
- Even 15 minutes helps
- Finish songs, even bad ones
- Quantity leads to quality
- Keep a songwriting journal

### Study Great Songs

**Analyze songs you love:**
- What's the structure?
- Where's the hook?
- What makes the melody work?
- How do lyrics and music connect?

### Get Feedback

**Share your work:**
- Find trusted listeners
- Ask specific questions
- Take criticism constructively
- Know when to ignore advice too

## Quick Start: Write a Song Today

**20-Minute Song Exercise:**

1. **Pick a title** (2 min)
   - Use a phrase you heard today
   - Or "The ___" + emotion/place/person

2. **Write the chorus** (8 min)
   - 4-6 lines that capture the title's meaning
   - Find a simple melody
   - This is your hook

3. **Write verse 1** (5 min)
   - Set up the story
   - Lead into the chorus
   - Different melody than chorus

4. **Basic structure** (5 min)
   - Verse 1 → Chorus → Verse 2 → Chorus
   - Done. You have a song.

**Refine later.** The point is to finish.
