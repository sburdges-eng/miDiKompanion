# Songwriting Exercises

Practical exercises to strengthen your songwriting skills.

---

## Daily Warm-Ups (5-15 minutes)

### Title Brainstorm

**Time:** 5 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Set a timer for 5 minutes
2. Write as many song titles as possible
3. Don't judge — just write
4. Aim for 15-20 titles
5. Star the ones that intrigue you

**Why it works:** Builds idea generation muscle.

---

### Melody Mumble

**Time:** 5 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Play a simple chord (or play backing track)
2. Hum or sing nonsense syllables
3. Follow the sounds that feel natural
4. Record with voice memo
5. Listen back for melody nuggets

**Why it works:** Bypasses the thinking brain.

---

### Object Writing

**Time:** 10 minutes (strict)

**Exercise:**
1. Pick a random object (coffee cup, doorknob, tree)
2. Set timer for exactly 10 minutes
3. Write continuously about sensory experiences
4. Use all senses: sight, sound, taste, touch, smell, movement, body
5. Stop when timer ends — even mid-sentence

**Why it works:** Expands descriptive ability, generates imagery.

**Example prompt:** "Morning coffee"
*Bitter on the tongue, steam rising like ghosts from the mug, ceramic warm between palms, the ritual of waiting...*

## Melody Exercises

### Melody Limitation

**Time:** 10-15 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Limit yourself to 4-5 notes only
2. Write a melody using just those notes
3. Create interest through rhythm, not pitch
4. See how much you can do with little

**Why it works:** Forces creativity through limitation.

---

### Sing the Opposite

**Time:** 10 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Hum a melody you know
2. Now sing the opposite direction
3. When it goes up, go down (and vice versa)
4. Keep the rhythm the same

**Why it works:** Breaks melodic habits.

---

### Rhythm First

**Time:** 10 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Create a rhythm by clapping or tapping
2. Now add melody to that exact rhythm
3. The rhythm dictates the melody shape

**Why it works:** Melody and rhythm connection.

---

### One-Note Melody

**Time:** 5 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Sing a phrase on ONE note only
2. Make it interesting through rhythm alone
3. Add a second note
4. See how few notes you need

**Why it works:** Shows rhythm's power.

---

## Lyric Exercises

### Rhyme Expansion

**Time:** 10 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Write down a word (e.g., "heart")
2. List every rhyme you can think of
3. Include near-rhymes and slant rhymes
4. Pick the most interesting, unexpected rhyme
5. Write a couplet using it

**Why it works:** Expands rhyme vocabulary.

---

### Cliché Flip

**Time:** 15 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Write down 5 cliché phrases:
   - "Love is a battlefield"
   - "Heart of gold"
   - "Walking on sunshine"
2. Flip each one — say the opposite or twist it:
   - "Love is a ceasefire"
   - "Heart of rust"
   - "Drowning in darkness"
3. Use the flip in a verse

**Why it works:** Avoids clichés, finds fresh imagery.

---

### Show Don't Tell

**Time:** 15 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Pick an emotion (sad, happy, angry, etc.)
2. Write 4 lines WITHOUT using that word
3. Use images, actions, and details instead

**Example (sad):**
Instead of: "I'm so sad"
Write: "Staring at your side of the bed / The coffee's cold, I can't get up"

**Why it works:** Creates vivid, specific lyrics.

---

### Constraint Writing

**Time:** 15 minutes

**Exercise:** Write a verse using ONE of these constraints:
- No words with the letter "E"
- Only one-syllable words
- Every line starts with the same letter
- No rhymes allowed
- Include a color in every line

**Why it works:** Constraints force creative solutions.

---

## Chord Exercises

### Four-Chord Challenge

**Time:** 15 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Pick any 4 chords
2. Play them in different orders
3. Write a verse AND chorus using only those 4 chords
4. Make sections sound different through melody/rhythm

**Why it works:** Proves you don't need complex harmony.

---

### Steal the Changes

**Time:** 15 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Pick a song you love
2. Write down its chord progression
3. Write a completely NEW melody over those chords
4. Make it unrecognizable as the original

**Why it works:** Chord progressions can't be copyrighted. Learning from the greats.

---

### Borrowed Chord

**Time:** 10 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Play a simple progression (I - IV - V - I)
2. Replace one chord with a "borrowed" chord from parallel minor
3. Notice how the emotion shifts
4. Write a melody that emphasizes that moment

**Example in C:** C - F - G - C → C - F - Ab - C

**Why it works:** Expands harmonic vocabulary.

---

## Structure Exercises

### Section Swap

**Time:** 20 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Write a short verse (4-8 lines)
2. Now write what comes AFTER it
3. Swap them — make the "after" part your verse
4. What was your verse becomes the chorus
5. Does it work? Why or why not?

**Why it works:** Challenges assumptions about order.

---

### Bridge First

**Time:** 15 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Start by writing a bridge
2. Make it emotionally different
3. Now write the verse and chorus that lead to it
4. The bridge is your destination

**Why it works:** Builds backward, ensures bridge matters.

---

### 60-Second Song

**Time:** 3 minutes (strict!)

**Exercise:**
1. Set timer for 3 minutes
2. Write and record a complete song (verse + chorus minimum)
3. Doesn't matter if it's bad
4. Just finish

**Why it works:** Defeats perfectionism, proves you can finish.

---

## Full Song Exercises

### Song in an Hour

**Time:** 60 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. **0-10 min:** Concept and title
2. **10-25 min:** Chorus (melody + rough lyrics)
3. **25-40 min:** Verse 1 (melody + lyrics)
4. **40-50 min:** Verse 2 (lyrics)
5. **50-60 min:** Refine and record demo

**Why it works:** Time pressure prevents overthinking.

---

### Song-A-Day Challenge

**Time:** 1 week to 1 month

**Exercise:**
1. Commit to writing one song per day
2. Can be incomplete, rough, terrible
3. Just write every day
4. At the end, review — some will have gems

**Variations:**
- FAWM (February Album Writing Month) — 14 songs in 28 days
- 50/90 — 50 songs in 90 days

**Why it works:** Volume creates quality.

---

### Rewrite a Classic

**Time:** 30 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Pick a song you love
2. Rewrite the lyrics completely
3. Keep the melody exactly the same
4. New theme, new story, new words

**Why it works:** Melody structure education.

---

### Answer Song

**Time:** 30 minutes

**Exercise:**
1. Pick a song (any song)
2. Write a response from another perspective
3. The other person in the relationship
4. Or a third party observing

**Why it works:** Story and perspective practice.

## Weekly Challenges

### Monday: Title Mining
- Find 10 titles from books, movies, overheard phrases
- Star your favorites

### Tuesday: Object Writing
- 10 minutes, random object
- Capture sensory details

### Wednesday: Melody Day
- Create 3 different melodies
- No lyrics, just melody

### Thursday: Lyric Day
- Write lyrics without melody
- Focus on story and imagery

### Friday: Full Song
- Put it all together
- Finish something

### Weekend: Review and Refine
- Listen to week's work
- Pick one to develop further

---

## Breaking Writer's Block

### When Stuck, Try:

1. **Change location** — New environment
2. **Change instrument** — Piano instead of guitar
3. **Change time** — Morning instead of night
4. **Lower stakes** — "This doesn't have to be good"
5. **Set a timer** — Urgency helps
6. **Collaborate** — Fresh perspective
7. **Constraint** — Add a limitation
8. **Input** — Read poetry, watch film, listen to new music
9. **Movement** — Walk, run, dance
10. **Sleep on it** — Return tomorrow
