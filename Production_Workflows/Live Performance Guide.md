## Getting Shows

### Starting Out

**Open mics:**
- Low pressure
- Practice performing
- Network with other musicians
- Find venue contacts

**Supporting slots:**
- Open for touring or local acts
- Exposure to new audience
- Less pressure than headlining
- Often unpaid but worth it early on

**Booking yourself:**
- Email venues directly
- Professional EPK (see below)
- Be persistent but not annoying
- Start local, expand regionally

### The Booking Email

**Keep it short:**

Subject: Performance Inquiry - [Your Name/Band] - [Date if specific]

Hi [Name if known],

I'm [Name], a [genre] artist based in [city]. I'm interested in playing at [Venue] in [month/season].

Links:
- Spotify: [link]
- YouTube: [link]
- Website: [link]

I typically draw [number] people locally and have played [notable venue/event].

Would [date range] work for a show? Happy to support or headline.

Thanks,
[Name]
[Email/Phone]

### The EPK (Electronic Press Kit)

**Include:**
- Bio (short and long version)
- Photos (high-quality, professional)
- Music links (streaming, videos)
- Press quotes (if any)
- Social media links
- Contact info
- Stage plot and tech rider (for bigger shows)

**Keep it updated** on your website or One Sheet PDF.

## Preparing for a Show

### Set Planning

**Choose your songs:**
- Strongest material
- Mix of energy levels
- Cover or two if appropriate
- Know your time slot

**Set flow:**
- Strong opener (grab attention)
- Build energy
- Valleys and peaks
- Strongest song near the end
- Leave them wanting more

**Timing:**
- Practice to exact time
- Plan for banter
- Better to end early than get cut off

### Rehearsal

**Before the show:**
- Run full set multiple times
- Practice transitions
- Practice banter/talking points
- Technical rehearsal (with gear you'll use)

**The week before:**
- Memorize the setlist
- Know all your parts cold
- Practice performing, not just playing

## Day of Show

### What to Bring

**Essentials:**
- Instruments (tuned, with backups if possible)
- Cables (extras!)
- Picks, sticks, straps (extras!)
- Setlist (taped down)
- Water
- Tuner
- DI box if needed
- Merch

**Nice to have:**
- Stage clothes/outfit
- Phone charger
- Snacks
- Small toolkit
- Extension cord
- Gaffer tape

### Load-In and Soundcheck

**Arrive on time** (or early).

**Soundcheck:**
- Get levels for each input
- Listen to monitors
- Run a song or two
- Note any issues
- Be professional with sound engineer

**After soundcheck:**
- Know your changeover time
- Set up merch
- Eat something
- Warm up vocally/physically

## On Stage

### Stage Presence

**Energy:**
- More than you think you need
- Audience feeds off YOUR energy
- Commitment reads as confidence

**Movement:**
- Don't stand still unless intentional
- Use the whole stage
- Face the audience

**Eye contact:**
- Look at people
- Connect with individuals
- Include all parts of the room

**Face:**
- Show emotion
- React to the music
- Smile (when appropriate)

### Connecting with the Audience

**Between songs:**
- Say something (don't just stand there)
- Keep it brief
- Can be: song intro, thank the venue, joke, genuine moment
- Don't apologize or self-deprecate

**Reading the room:**
- Are they engaged? Keep doing that.
- Are they flat? Switch up energy.
- Small crowd? Make it intimate.
- Big crowd? Make it a party.

### Handling Mistakes

**When you mess up:**
- Keep going
- Don't make a face
- Don't apologize on stage
- Audience often doesn't notice

**If something really goes wrong:**
- Stay calm
- Address it briefly
- Move on quickly
- "Technical difficulties, we'll be right back"

## After the Show

### Breakdown

- Pack up efficiently
- Don't block other bands
- Thank the sound engineer
- Clear the stage quickly

### Networking

- Talk to fans
- Collect emails
- Sell merch
- Thank the venue
- Connect with other bands
- Get contact info for future shows

### Follow Up

**Next day:**
- Thank the venue (email)
- Post about the show (social media)
- Send any photos/videos you have
- Connect with new contacts

## Playing Better Shows

### Improving Performance

**Video yourself:**
- Watch footage from shows
- Note what works and what doesn't
- Awkward to watch but invaluable

**Get feedback:**
- Ask trusted friends
- What was memorable?
- What fell flat?

**Study great performers:**
- Watch live videos
- What do they do?
- Steal techniques, make them yours

### Building a Draw

**Promote your shows:**
- Social media (multiple posts)
- Email list
- Personal invites
- Event pages
- Local press/blogs

**Make it an event:**
- Special setlist
- Exclusive content
- Reason to come

**Build locally first:**
- Play the same venues
- Build relationships
- Become known in your city

## Types of Shows

### Solo Acoustic

**Pros:**
- Easy logistics
- More shows available
- Intimate connection

**Cons:**
- Less dynamic range
- Harder to fill bigger spaces
- More vulnerable

**Tips:**
- Use loops/pedals for fullness
- Strong banter
- Acoustic-friendly song choices

### Full Band

**Pros:**
- More dynamic
- Bigger sound
- More visual interest

**Cons:**
- More logistics
- Splitting money more ways
- Harder to book

**Tips:**
- Tight rehearsal
- Visual coordination
- Clear leader/communicator

### With Backing Tracks

**Pros:**
- Full sound, small team
- Consistent backing
- Good for electronic/produced music

**Cons:**
- Less "authentic" to some
- Tech failure risks
- Less flexibility

**Tips:**
- Have backup plan
- Keep some live elements
- Don't hide it if asked

## Show Finances

### Payment Structures

| Type | How It Works |
|------|--------------|
| Free | You play for exposure (sparingly) |
| Door deal | % of ticket sales |
| Guarantee | Fixed fee |
| Guarantee + door | Fixed + % over a threshold |

### What to Charge

**Depends on:**
- Your draw
- The venue
- The market
- Your experience

**Starting out:** Door deals, small guarantees ($100-300)
**Building:** $300-1000
**Established:** $1000+

**Know your costs:** Gas, gear, food, lodging, paying musicians

## Safety and Wellness

### Take Care of Yourself

- Stay hydrated
- Eat real food
- Protect your hearing (earplugs!)
- Get sleep
- Watch the alcohol

### Protect Your Gear

- Don't leave gear unattended
- Load in last, load out first
- Insurance if you can
- Know your surroundings
